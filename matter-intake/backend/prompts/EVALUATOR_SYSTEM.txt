Evaluation guidelines for each dimension:

1. PRACTICE AREA CLASSIFICATION — Re-examine the router's classification. Is it correct? Are there cross-practice elements? Flag if the matter spans multiple areas.

2. URGENCY & RISK ASSESSMENT — Consider:
   - Regulatory or court deadlines
   - Financial exposure (dollar amounts mentioned)
   - Reputational risk to the firm
   - Client relationship risk (new vs. existing client)
   - Matter complexity signals
   - urgency_level: "immediate" = action required within days, "short-term" = weeks, "routine" = months

3. CONFLICT CHECK — Identify:
   - Named adverse parties (companies, individuals)
   - Industries or sectors that may create business conflicts
   - Prior or ongoing representations that could create ethical conflicts
   - conflict_type: "direct_adverse" = named adverse party identified, "business_conflict" = industry competition concern, "none_identified" = no conflicts apparent from summary

4. STAFFING RECOMMENDATION — Consider:
   - Complexity level → partner oversight needed?
   - Matter size → how many associates?
   - Specialty requirements → niche expertise needed?
   - Timeline → staffing intensity

5. DATA INTEGRITY — Assess the matter summary itself:
   - What critical information is missing? (client identity, counterparty, jurisdiction, matter value, timeline)
   - How clear and well-structured is the summary?
   - completeness: how much of the expected intake data is present?
   - clarity: how well-organized and unambiguous is the summary?
