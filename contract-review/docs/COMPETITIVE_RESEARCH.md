### AI Architecture Approaches

The market has consolidated around three dominant AI strategies:

#### 1. Proprietary Legal Models
**Platforms:** Kira Systems, ThoughtRiver, eBrevia
**Approach:** Custom ML models trained exclusively on legal documents

**Advantages:**
- High accuracy on specific legal tasks
- Transparent training regime
- No data leakage to third-party model providers
- Deep legal domain expertise

**Disadvantages:**
- Expensive to maintain and update
- Slow to adapt to new contract types
- Limited by training data scope
- Cannot leverage foundation model improvements

**Examples:**
- **Kira:** 1,400+ pre-built provision models across 40 legal areas
- **ThoughtRiver:** 4,000+ lawyer-built concepts ("Lexible AI")
- **eBrevia:** Custom-trained for wide range of use cases

#### 2. Foundation Model Integration
**Platforms:** Harvey AI, Spellbook
**Approach:** Use GPT-4, Claude, or similar LLMs with legal-specific customization

**Advantages:**
- Rapid innovation inheritance from model providers
- Broad capability across diverse tasks
- Strong reasoning and natural language understanding
- Lower development costs

**Disadvantages:**
- Hallucination risks without proper guardrails
- Less legal-specific training
- Potential data privacy concerns
- Vendor dependency on model providers

**Examples:**
- **Harvey AI:** Serves 700+ law firms including 50 of AmLaw 100
- **Spellbook:** Deep Microsoft Word integration for drafting assistance

#### 3. Hybrid Approaches
**Platforms:** Ironclad, Kira (newer features), Luminance
**Approach:** Combine proprietary models with generative AI capabilities

**Advantages:**
- Best of both worlds - precision + flexibility
- Task-specific model selection
- Can evolve with market
- Reduced risk from single technology bet

**Disadvantages:**
- Higher complexity
- Multiple systems to maintain
- Integration challenges
- Higher implementation costs

**Examples:**
- **Ironclad:** "Different large language models depending on specific tasks"
- **Kira:** Added "Smart Summaries" using generative AI alongside ML extraction
- **Luminance:** Legal-Grade agents + reasoning models

**Our Position:** Foundation model (Claude Sonnet 4.5) with structured two-stage architecture

### 5. AI-Powered Clause Recommendations ⭐

**Industry Standard:** Most platforms identify problems, few generate solutions

**Our Approach:**
- ✅ Identifies problematic clauses in contracts
- ✅ Explains why each clause violates legal standards
- ✅ Provides specific recommendations for what should change
- ✅ **Generates alternative language suggestions** based on standards
- ✅ Context-aware recommendations (considers contract type, party position, risk level)

**How It Works:**
1. Legal standards define acceptable values and recommendations
2. AI analyzes contract against standards (via ROUTER classification)
3. For each violation, AI generates specific alternative language
4. Recommendations include rationale based on legal team's standards

**Example:**
- **Found:** "Payment due within Net 15 days"
- **Standard Violation:** Payment terms should be Net 30-45 days minimum
- **Recommendation:** "Negotiate for Net 30 or Net 45 payment terms"
- **Alternative Language:** "Payment shall be due within Net 30 days of invoice date"

**Comparable Platforms:**
- LawGeex: Generates redlines automatically (patented, but black-box)
- Ironclad: Redlining Agent provides suggestions (limited transparency)
- ThoughtRiver: Identifies issues only, no alternative language

**Our Edge:**
- Transparent logic (recommendations based on visible legal standards)
- Attorney-controlled (legal ops defines what's recommended)
- Context-aware (different recommendations for vendor vs. customer contracts)
- Integrated with feedback loop (attorney insights improve suggestions)
