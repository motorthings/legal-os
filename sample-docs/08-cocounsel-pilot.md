# Meridian Law Group — CoCounsel Pilot Evaluation

**Pilot Period:** October 2025 — January 2026
**Participant:** 1 Partner (Litigation), 2 Associates (Litigation)
**Tool:** Thomson Reuters CoCounsel (1 license)
**Use Cases:** Deposition summarization, document review for discovery

## Pilot Summary

### Usage
- 12 depositions summarized (average 200 pages each)
- 3 document review projects (~5,000 documents total)
- Estimated time savings: 45 hours over 3 months (~15 hours/month)
- Partner estimate: "Summaries were 80% as good as a first-year associate's first pass — good enough to start from, not good enough to file."

### What Worked
- Deposition summaries were structured and comprehensive
- Document review flagged relevant passages that associates confirmed were important
- Associates reported the tool was "easy to learn" (2-3 hours to basic proficiency)
- Saved approximately 12 hours on one large document review (200 hours → 188 hours, plus associate review)

### What Didn't Work
- 3 instances of hallucinated deposition testimony (AI invented statements not in the transcript) — caught in review
- Tool struggled with technical patent language in one case; outputs were "unusable"
- No integration with NetDocuments — documents had to be downloaded and re-uploaded
- Single license meant only one person could use it at a time
- No mobile access; partner wanted to review summaries on iPad during travel

### Cost Analysis
- CoCounsel license: $350/month
- Estimated time saved: ~45 hours × $350/hour blended rate = $15,750 value
- Net benefit (3 months): ~$14,700 (ignoring review time)
- If firm-wide: 10 licenses × $350 × 12 = $42,000/year

### Recommendations from Pilot Team
1. Expand pilot to 5 licenses (Corporate M&A + IP practice groups)
2. Integrate with NetDocuments before wider rollout (download/re-upload is the #1 friction point)
3. Create a 1-page "CoCounsel Quick Start" guide for new users
4. Establish review protocol: AI output is "first draft only" — must be verified line-by-line before use
5. Track time saved vs. time spent reviewing to build ROI case

## Current Status
Pilot completed January 2026. Recommendation to expand sitting with Managing Partner — not yet decided. Budget request for 5 additional licenses ($1,750/month) submitted but not approved.

## Firm Reaction (anecdotal)
- Litigation partner: "It's not magic but it saved me a weekend."
- Associate 1: "I'd rather review an AI summary than write one from scratch."
- Associate 2: "The depo summaries missed some nuance but caught things I would have skimmed past."
- Corporate partner (not in pilot): "I want to see it work on a deal document before I trust it."
- Employment partner (not in pilot): "Employment depos are different. I'm skeptical."
- Managing Partner: "Promising, but one pilot doesn't justify firm-wide spend. Show me more data."
