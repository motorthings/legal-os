# Meridian Law Group — Delegation & Approval Authority

**Status:** Informal — Not Codified
**Last Discussed:** Partner Retreat, October 2025

## Current State

Meridian Law Group operates under a traditional law firm governance model. The 3-member Partner Committee (Managing Partner + 2 senior partners) retains all significant decision authority. There is no written delegation framework.

### Decisions Requiring Managing Partner Approval (current, informal)
- New matter acceptance (all matters, regardless of size or complexity)
- Engagement letter terms and fee arrangements
- Conflict waivers
- Settlement authority above $50,000
- Hiring (attorneys and staff)
- Technology purchases above $5,000
- Press or external communications
- Pro bono commitments
- Any expenditure above $10,000

### What Associates Can Do Without Approval
- Conduct legal research
- Draft documents for partner review
- Communicate with clients on scheduling (not substance)
- File routine court documents (after partner sign-off)
- Attend depositions and hearings (with partner present)

### Pain Points
- Managing Partner reviews approximately 60-80 decisions per week
- Average response time for matter acceptance: 1.7 business days
- 2 matters lost to competitors in 2025 due to slow intake approval
- Associates report frustration: "I can't even order a Westlaw research package without approval"
- Partner Committee acknowledges the bottleneck but has not delegated authority

## Proposed Delegation Framework (DRAFT — Not Approved)

### Matter Acceptance
- **Routine matters** (standard corporate filings, simple employment matters, small litigation under $100K exposure): Practice Group Lead may accept without Managing Partner review
- **Standard matters** ($100K-$1M exposure, or new client relationships): Practice Group Lead + one other partner concurrence
- **Complex matters** ($1M+ exposure, multi-jurisdiction, or Fortune 500 clients): Managing Partner approval required

### Financial Authority
- Practice Group Leads may approve expenditures up to $25,000 within their group budget
- Associates may incur research and filing costs up to $500 without approval
- Technology purchases follow IT Manager recommendation + Managing Partner approval (unchanged)

### Settlement Authority
- Settlements up to $100,000: Practice Group Lead may approve
- Settlements $100,000-$500,000: Two partners must concur
- Settlements above $500,000: Full Partner Committee approval

### AI-Specific Delegation
- Associates may use firm-approved AI tools without prior approval per matter
- New AI tool evaluation: IT Manager may initiate trial without Partner Committee approval (currently requires Managing Partner sign-off)
- AI tool procurement: unchanged (Managing Partner approval for any purchase)

## Current Blockers to Implementation
1. Managing Partner has not formally approved the framework — concerns about "loss of control"
2. No system to track delegated decisions (currently all tracked in Managing Partner's inbox)
3. Some Practice Group Leads don't want the additional responsibility
4. Insurance carrier may need notification of delegation changes
5. No audit mechanism to review delegated decisions for quality

## Related: Ethical Walls & Confidentiality
- Ethical walls are currently implemented ad-hoc: the Managing Partner emails affected attorneys with "do not discuss" instructions
- No system-based enforcement (document access restrictions, email controls)
- 2 minor wall breaches in 2024 (information appeared in shared drive accessible to walled-off attorneys)
- Proposal to implement NetDocuments ethical wall features exists but is not prioritized
