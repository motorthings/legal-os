# Meridian Law Group — Knowledge Management Guidelines

**Status:** DRAFT — Not Yet Approved
**Author:** Corporate M&A Partner (self-appointed)
**Date:** September 2025

## Purpose
These guidelines describe how Meridian Law Group attorneys should organize, store, and share work product, research, templates, and institutional knowledge.

## Current Practices (to be replaced)

Presently, KM practices vary by practice group and individual attorney:

- **Corporate M&A:** Maintains a shared drive of templates organized by deal type. Most active KM practice in the firm.
- **Litigation:** Each partner maintains their own brief bank and motion templates. No shared repository. "Ask someone who's done one" is the primary KM method.
- **Employment:** Some templates on SharePoint. No regular updating. Several employment law updates circulated by email but not archived or searchable.
- **IP:** Patent templates organized by technology area. Trademark and copyright materials are scattered.

## Proposed Standards

### Document Naming
- All firm work product stored in NetDocuments (not local drives or email)
- Naming convention: `[Client-MatterID] [Document Type] [Date YYYY-MM-DD] [Version]`
- Example: `ACME-2024-0174 Purchase Agreement 2025-03-15 v2.docx`

### Template Management
- Practice group leads designate one "template owner" per practice area
- Templates reviewed and updated quarterly
- Outdated templates archived with "DEPRECATED" prefix, never deleted
- Template version history maintained in NetDocuments

### Research & Precedent
- Legal research memos saved to NetDocuments with matter tag and practice area tag
- Key case briefs and analysis tagged for firm-wide search
- "Go-by" documents for common transactions linked to template library
- Quarterly practice group KM review: what did we learn, what should everyone know?

### Search & Discovery
- All attorneys must complete NetDocuments search training (1 hour)
- Practice group-specific taxonomies to be developed
- SharePoint KM library to be migrated to NetDocuments or decommissioned

## Current Gaps (acknowledged)

1. **No dedicated KM staff.** KM is "extra credit" work, not anyone's responsibility.
2. **No enforcement mechanism.** These guidelines are aspirational — no one checks compliance.
3. **Litigation and Employment not yet on NetDocuments.** Can't standardize KM until the migration is complete.
4. **No metadata standards.** Documents are stored but not tagged consistently — search remains unreliable.
5. **Partner precedent hoarding.** Several partners keep their best work product on personal drives, "just in case."
6. **No budget.** KM improvements are unfunded in 2026 budget.
