# Meridian Law Group — Matter Intake Process

## Current State (as of January 2026)

### Intake Flow (varies by partner)
1. Prospective client contacts partner directly (phone, email, referral)
2. Partner assesses fit and conflicts informally — often from memory
3. If partner approves, assistant creates matter in Clio with basic info
4. Engagement letter sent (templates vary by partner, some still use Word docs from 2018)
5. Conflict check: assistant emails Managing Partner's assistant with party names; response typically 1-3 business days
6. Matter opened in Clio once conflicts cleared

### Known Issues
- No standard intake form — information collected varies drastically by partner
- 3 matters opened in 2025 with conflict issues discovered after work began (all resolved, but embarrassing)
- Average intake-to-open time: 4.7 business days (target: 1 day)
- No data validation at intake — duplicate client records, inconsistent naming
- Practice area assignment is inconsistent; some matters get assigned to wrong group initially
- No early risk assessment — matters are accepted before complexity is understood
- Billing arrangements not consistently documented at intake

## Proposed Target State (Q2 2026)

### Standardized Intake Form (all channels)
- Client name, contacts, adverse parties
- Jurisdiction(s)
- Practice area (validated dropdown — no free text)
- Matter type (standardized taxonomy TBD)
- Estimated complexity (low/medium/high — with definitions)
- Billing arrangement (hourly/flat/contingency/mixed)
- Estimated budget range
- Key deadlines
- Referral source
- Conflict check required (yes/no — auto-flagged for adverse party entries)

### Automated Conflict Check
- Integrate Clio with conflict database
- Automated search against existing and former clients, adverse parties
- Flag potential conflicts for Managing Partner review
- Target: clear simple conflicts same-day, complex within 48 hours

### Intake Triage
- Routine matters (standard corp filings, simple employment) → associate-led with partner sign-off
- Standard matters (mid-complexity litigation, mid-size M&A) → partner review within 48 hours
- Complex matters (multi-jurisdiction, Fortune 500 clients, high exposure) → full partner committee review

### Success Metrics
- Average intake-to-open: under 1 business day
- Zero conflicts discovered after work begins
- 100% of matters have complete intake data at opening
- Billing arrangement documented before work begins: 100%

## Current Blockers
- Managing Partner has not approved the standardized intake form design
- No budget allocated for Clio conflict-check module integration
- Partner resistance to "bureaucratic" intake forms — cultural issue
- IT bandwidth limited (NetDocuments migration is priority)
