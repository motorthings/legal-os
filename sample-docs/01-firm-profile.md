# Meridian Law Group — Firm Profile

## Overview
Meridian Law Group is a mid-size full-service law firm with 47 attorneys across 4 practice groups:
- **Corporate M&A** (14 attorneys, 3 partners)
- **Litigation** (16 attorneys, 4 partners)
- **Employment Law** (9 attorneys, 2 partners)
- **Intellectual Property** (8 attorneys, 2 partners)

Founded in 2003. Offices in Chicago and Milwaukee. 22 support staff (paralegals, legal assistants, admin).

## Key Clients
- 3 Fortune 500 manufacturing clients (primary corporate work)
- 12 mid-market technology companies (IP and employment)
- Regional hospital system (employment and litigation)
- Various small business and individual clients

## Annual Metrics (2025)
- 340 new matters opened
- $18.2M gross revenue
- 1,850 billable hours average per attorney
- 78% realization rate
- Primary billing: hourly (85%), flat fee (10%), contingency (5%)

## Current Technology Stack
- **Practice Management:** Clio Manage (adopted 2021)
- **Document Management:** NetDocuments (adopted 2023, migrating from network drives)
- **Billing:** Clio billing module + QuickBooks for accounting
- **Email/Calendar:** Microsoft 365 (Exchange Online)
- **Communication:** Microsoft Teams, email primarily
- **Legal Research:** Westlaw, LexisNexis
- **CRM:** None — partners maintain personal contact lists in Outlook
- **Knowledge Management:** SharePoint document library (underutilized), individual partner collections
- **AI Tools:** 3 attorneys experimenting with ChatGPT; 1 partner using CoCounsel for doc review on a trial basis

## Known Pain Points
- NetDocuments migration only 60% complete — Corporate and IP mostly migrated, Litigation and Employment still on network drives
- No standard matter intake process — each partner handles intake differently
- Conflict checks are manual (email to managing partner's assistant)
- 3 partners handle all major decisions; associates have no delegation authority
- No formal training program for technology or AI
- SharePoint KM library has inconsistent tagging, no search optimization
- Billing descriptions inconsistent across attorneys — realization rate suffering
- Attorneys report spending 30% of research time re-finding documents they know exist somewhere

## Strategic Goals (2026-2027)
- Complete NetDocuments migration firm-wide
- Standardize matter intake and conflict checking
- Evaluate AI tools for contract review and due diligence
- Improve realization rate to 85%+
- Develop associate delegation framework
- Implement CRM system
- Create formal KM program with dedicated staffing
