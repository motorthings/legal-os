# Meridian Law Group — AI Usage Policy

**Effective Date:** March 15, 2025
**Version:** 1.0
**Approved By:** Managing Partner

## 1. Purpose
This policy governs the use of artificial intelligence tools, including large language models, AI-assisted legal research, and AI-enabled document review, by all attorneys, paralegals, and staff of Meridian Law Group.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all firm personnel and all AI tools used in connection with client matters, firm operations, or professional development.

## 3. General Principles

3.1 **Attorney Responsibility.** AI-generated content, analysis, or recommendations are not a substitute for attorney judgment. The responsible attorney MUST review and verify all AI outputs before use in any client matter.

3.2 **Confidentiality.** Under no circumstances may attorneys input client-confidential information, privileged communications, or non-public case strategy into public AI systems (including ChatGPT, Google Gemini, or similar consumer tools). Only firm-approved AI tools with contractual data protection guarantees may receive client data.

3.3 **Accuracy.** AI tools may produce incorrect, incomplete, or fabricated content. All citations, case references, statutory interpretations, and factual claims generated by AI must be independently verified against primary sources.

3.4 **Billing.** Time spent reviewing and correcting AI outputs is billable. Time saved by AI assistance must be reflected in adjusted billing — the firm does not bill for AI-generated work product at the same rate as attorney-authored work without disclosure and client consent.

## 4. Approved AI Tools

| Tool | Use Case | Data Protection |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| CoCounsel (pilot) | Document review, deposition summaries | Contractual — client data permitted |
| Westlaw AI | Legal research assistance | Westlaw terms — research queries only |
| Microsoft 365 Copilot (evaluating) | Email summarization, document drafting | Covered under M365 E5 agreement |

## 5. Prohibited Uses

- Inputting client names, matter details, financial terms, or settlement amounts into public AI chatbots
- Using AI-generated legal documents without attorney review
- Relying on AI for ethical wall determinations or conflict analysis
- Using AI to generate client communications without review and approval

## 6. Training Requirement
All attorneys must complete the 1-hour "AI in Legal Practice" CLE module by June 30, 2025. New hires must complete within 30 days.

## 7. Incident Reporting
Any unintended disclosure of client information via AI tools must be reported to the Managing Partner and the firm's ethics counsel within 24 hours.

## 8. Policy Review
This policy will be reviewed quarterly. The next review is scheduled for June 2025.
