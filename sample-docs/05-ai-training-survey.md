# Meridian Law Group — AI Training & Awareness Survey Results

**Survey Date:** November 2025
**Respondents:** 41 of 47 attorneys (87% response rate)
**Conducted By:** IT Manager, at Managing Partner's request

## Summary Findings

### AI Tool Usage (current)
| Tool | Never Used | Tried Once | Occasional Use | Regular Use |
|------|-----------|------------|----------------|-------------|
| ChatGPT / consumer AI | 34% | 27% | 29% | 10% |
| Westlaw AI features | 61% | 17% | 15% | 7% |
| CoCounsel | 95% | 5% | 0% | 0% |
| Microsoft Copilot | 88% | 7% | 5% | 0% |
| Any AI legal research tool | 56% | 22% | 15% | 7% |

### AI Knowledge Self-Assessment
- "I understand how AI language models work": 12% agree, 54% disagree, 34% unsure
- "I can distinguish good AI output from bad": 22% agree, 41% disagree, 37% unsure
- "I know which AI tools are safe for client data": 29% agree, 44% disagree, 27% unsure
- "I would use AI more if I had training": 71% agree, 10% disagree, 19% unsure

### Attitudes Toward AI
- "AI will make lawyers more efficient": 63% agree, 15% disagree, 22% unsure
- "AI threatens the billable hour model": 49% agree, 24% disagree, 27% unsure
- "AI will reduce the need for junior associates": 54% agree, 20% disagree, 26% unsure
- "My clients expect us to use modern technology": 78% agree, 8% disagree, 14% unsure
- "I'm excited about AI in legal practice": 34% agree, 22% neutral, 44% concerned
- "I worry about being left behind on AI": 51% agree, 27% disagree, 22% unsure

### Training Preferences
Respondents were asked: "What would help you adopt AI tools?" (select all that apply)

| Option | % Selected |
|--------|-----------|
| Hands-on workshop with real legal examples | 78% |
| CLE-accredited AI course | 63% |
| One-on-one coaching from someone who uses AI well | 56% |
| Written guides with step-by-step instructions | 49% |
| Firm-provided AI tools with built-in training | 71% |
| Peer demonstration (see a colleague use it) | 44% |
| Nothing — I'm not interested | 7% |

### Key Quotes (anonymized)
- "I tried ChatGPT for a research question and it made up three cases. I haven't touched AI since."
- "The younger associates use it but won't admit it because they think partners will disapprove."
- "I don't know what I'm allowed to use and what I'm not. The policy says 'approved tools' but nobody told me which ones are approved."
- "Our clients are using AI. If we don't, they'll hire firms that do."
- "I bill 2,000 hours. When am I supposed to learn a new tool?"

## Recommended Actions (IT Manager)
1. Schedule firm-wide AI training workshop (2 hours, hands-on)
2. Publish and distribute approved tools list with clear use cases
3. Identify 2-3 "AI champions" in each practice group for peer support
4. Provide CLE credit for AI training completion
5. Create a Teams channel for AI questions and tips
6. Address billable hour anxiety directly — leadership communication needed
