# AI Literacy Workshop — 60-Minute Practice Group Session

## Audience
Partners, associates, and legal professionals in a specific practice group.
Assume technical sophistication in law, zero in AI.

## Session Structure

### 0:00-0:05 — Opening: Why We're Here
- The firm has deployed AI tools. They only create value if you use them.
- 69% of lawyers use AI individually. 43% of firms have no AI policy.
- This firm is in the 34% that provides governed, secure, auditable AI.
- Today: what it can do, what it can't, how to use it safely.

### 0:05-0:15 — What Is This Thing? (LLM Basics, No Math)
- An LLM is a pattern-matching engine trained on text, not a legal database.
- It predicts what word comes next — brilliantly, but still predicting.
- It does not "know" anything. It does not "understand" your matter.
- It has no memory of your previous conversations (unless the platform retains them).
- Key distinction: Harvey (enterprise, governed, ZDR) vs. ChatGPT (consumer, trains on your input).

### 0:15-0:25 — What It's Good At (With Examples From Your Practice)
- Summarizing: "Read this 80-page deposition and give me the 10 key admissions."
- Extracting: "Pull every change-of-control clause from these 50 supply agreements."
- Drafting: "Write a first draft of a response to this demand letter."
- Comparing: "What's different between the July and September versions of this contract?"
- Searching: "Have we ever argued that a force majeure clause covers a pandemic?"

### 0:25-0:35 — What It's Bad At (And Dangerous At)
- It hallucinates. It will fabricate case citations that look completely real.
- It cannot count, do math reliably, or reason about time.
- It has no judgment about your specific client, matter, or strategy.
- It doesn't know what it doesn't know — it always sounds confident.
- Rule: AI output is a first draft. Your judgment is the final draft. Never submit AI output without review.

### 0:35-0:45 — The Safety Boundaries (Non-Negotiable)
- Never put client names, matter numbers, or identifying details into a consumer AI tool.
- Never paste a full contract into a personal ChatGPT tab.
- Use only firm-approved tools (Harvey, governed internal tools).
- If you're unsure whether a tool is approved, ask before you use it.
- Confidentiality isn't a policy — it's the architecture. The firm's tools enforce it. Consumer tools don't.

### 0:45-0:55 — Hands-On: Your First Prompt
- Live demo with a sanitized example from the practice group's actual work.
- Show: bad prompt → mediocre output. Good prompt → useful output.
- The difference: context, specificity, and "act as a [role]" framing.
- Each participant writes and runs one prompt relevant to their current work.

### 0:55-0:60 — Q&A + Next Steps
- Champion network: who in this practice group is the go-to for AI questions.
- Office hours: when and where to get hands-on help.
- Feedback channel: how to report issues, suggest improvements, request capabilities.
